The rising sun painted the baked-brick walls of Mohenjo-daro a warm orange. Maya, a young potter's apprentice, hurried through the grid-like streets, her bare feet padding softly on the well-worn stone. The air hummed with the sounds of life – the rhythmic clang of metalworkers, the chattering of merchants in the bustling marketplace, the distant bleating of goats. The Great Bath beckoned, its cool water promising respite from the desert heat. Today, Maya hoped to impress her master with her newest vessel, a clay jar painted with intricate fish scales, mirroring the bounty the Indus River provided for their thriving city. Life was predictable, organized, and secure within Mohenjo-daro's well-planned walls.
